LEXEMS AND SEPARATORS

The language's lexems consist of the alphabet symbols. The lexems are separated with one or several separators.

The separators are:

  • space;
  • horizontal tabulation sign;
  • end of line;
  • end of object (file);
  • comment.

The separators may follow one another, any number and any combination (except the end of object sign).

A comment may be any text in figure parentheses. That text may contain separators (except the end of object sign) or symbols not belonging to the alphabet. There can not be comments within a comment, nor any figure brackets.
