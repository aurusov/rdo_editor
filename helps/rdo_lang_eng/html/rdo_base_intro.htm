PRIMARY CONSTRUCTIONS OF RAO LANGUAGE

The following denotations are used for describing the language constructions.

Each inscription rule consists of one or several alternatives (phrases) divided by vertical dash ( | ).

A phrase may contain zero or more elements.

An element is either the language's reserved word (distinguished by Bold font), or a special symbol (combination), or some metaname in angular parentheses  <>, or another syntactic expression bound at both sides with figure {}, square [] or round () parentheses.

Figure parentheses signify the repetition (zero or more entries), and round parentheses signify the grouping (accurately one entry) of the expressions they embrace.

Language constructions have a grey background.
