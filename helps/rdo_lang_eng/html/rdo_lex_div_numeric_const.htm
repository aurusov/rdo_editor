NUMERICAL CONSTANTS

Numerical constants are written just like they are written in higher level programming languages.

Integer constants are presented as digital sequences with "+" or "-" sign before them.

Real constants include the entire and fraction parts divided with decimal point, with decimal degree possibly following. The degree starts with "E" or "e" symbol followed by the degree value (signed integer constant). The real constant itself may have a sign too.

Remarks

For integer constants, the allowed range is from -32767 to 32767; for long integers it is from -2147483647 to 2147483647.

Real constants are stored in 4-byte-long format (ranging from 1.5E-45 to 3.4E+38, with accuracy up to 7 or 8 decimal characters).

Examples

12345
-12345
123.45E6
-12.3e-4
0.012345
+12345
